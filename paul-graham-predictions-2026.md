# Paul Graham on Vuen AI — March 2026

> "The best startups look like toys at first. Vuen doesn't even look like a toy — it looks like the future someone forgot to ship five years ago."

## Top 10 Things PG Would Say About This Site

### 1. Voice-First is the Right Bet, Finally

"Every few years someone tries voice interfaces and fails. The difference now is LLMs actually understand what you mean. Vuen is riding the right wave at the right time. The founders who win are the ones who try an idea *when the infrastructure finally supports it*, not when it's trendy."

### 2. They're Replacing Dashboards, Not Improving Them

"The most dangerous startups don't make a better version of the existing thing. They make the existing thing unnecessary. Nobody *wants* a dashboard. They want answers. Vuen figured that out. The best product is the one that eliminates the category it's in."

### 3. The Pricing Shows Confidence

"Free tier to get you hooked, $200/month Pro tier — that's real B2B pricing. Not $9/month indie-hacker pricing that signals you don't believe your own product. When you charge $200/month, you're saying: this replaces a BI analyst's afternoon. That's worth funding."

### 4. The Landing Page is Doing Too Much

"Seventeen sections. Six languages. Custom cursor effects. Glass morphism. This is a classic YC mistake — spending months on the marketing site instead of talking to users. The best landing pages I've seen at YC had three sections: what it does, a demo, and a signup button. Ship the product, not the website."

### 5. Enterprise Features Too Early is a Red Flag

"SSO/SAML, on-premise deployment, SLA guarantees — on a waitlist product? This tells me the founders are building what they *think* enterprises want instead of what the first 10 users actually need. At this stage, you should be embarrassed by how manual everything is. Do things that don't scale."

### 6. The Real Moat is Data Connections

"Voice is a UI layer. Charts are a UI layer. The hard part — the part that makes this defensible — is connecting to messy SQL databases, dirty CSVs, and janky APIs and making them *just work*. If Vuen nails the data connection layer, the voice and visualization parts become a nice-to-have on top of a must-have."

### 7. Real Estate Vertical is Smart

"The best way to grow a horizontal product is to start vertical. Real estate brokers are data-rich, tech-poor, and have money. They'll pay $200/month without blinking if you save them from Excel. Win one vertical completely before going broad."

### 8. The Orb is Memorable

"Most B2B SaaS looks like it was designed by a committee. The 3D orb that breathes and listens — that's a brand. People will say 'ask the orb' the way they say 'Google it.' Never underestimate the power of a product that people *want* to show their friends."

### 9. Internationalization is Premature

"Six languages before product-market fit. This is a classic 'building for scale before you have anyone to scale to' mistake. Find 100 users who love you in English first. Then translate."

### 10. The Biggest Risk is Obvious

"Every big player — Microsoft, Google, Salesforce — is bolting AI onto their BI tools right now. Vuen's advantage is speed and focus. The moment they slow down to add enterprise checklists, Copilot catches up. The only strategy is to be so much better for the first 1000 users that switching costs become real."

---

## The Verdict

"In summary: strong idea, strong timing, strong pricing. But the founders need to stop polishing the landing page and go find 10 users who would be genuinely angry if Vuen disappeared. That's when you know you've won."

— Paul Graham, March 2026
